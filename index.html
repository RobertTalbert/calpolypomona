How she determines whether her study is effective: ∅

Betty

Starts several days before to plan her upcoming study session --- topics, methods, physical space, schedule

Sets specific goals for study and for performance

Has a means of knowing whether her study is effective

Maintains awareness of her actions and behaviors, makes changes if necessary

Maintains awareness of affect --- allows good feelings for success and converts failures into improvements

Later on, evaluates her efforts

Self Regulation

Self-regulation is not a mental ability or an academic performance skill; rather it is the self-directive process by which learners transform their mental abilities into academic skills.

[SRL] involves the self-awareness, self-motivation, and behavioral skill to implement that knowledge appropriately.

[SRL] involves the selective use of specific processes that must be personally adapted to each learning task.

Zimmerman, B.J. (2002). Becoming a self-regulated learner: An overview. Theory Into Practice, 41 (2), 64-70.

What does the research tell us about SRL?

Use of self-regulatory processes correlates strongly with academic achievement and standardized test performance. (Zimmerman & Martinez-Pons, 1986)

Students who set "proximal goals" are more likely to self-observe their performance, more likely to achieve in the target areas, and will display higher levels of self-efficacy. (Bandura & Schunk, 1981)

Simply asking students to self-record some aspect of their learning can lead to “spontaneous” improvements in functioning (Shapiro, 1984)

Few teachers effectively prepare students to learn on their own. Students are seldom given a choice regarding academic tasks to pursue, methods for carrying out complex assignments, or study partners. Few teachers encourage students to establish specific goals for their academic work or estimate their competence on new tasks.

(Zimmerman, 2002)

  1. The proposition is false.
  2. The proposition is true but the proof has a fatal flaw.
  3. The proposition is true and the proof has a significant but non-fatal flaw.
  4. The proposition and proof are OK.
